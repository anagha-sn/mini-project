The Problem

Students Are Lost
Without the Right Guidance

Career decisions are life-changing — yet most students face them with limited information, societal pressure, and no personalized support.

😕

Confusion & Pressure

Students are overwhelmed by choices and influenced by peers and family rather than data-driven insights aligned to their actual strengths.

🔍

Scattered Information

Exam details, college rankings, fees, and placement data are scattered across dozens of platforms — forcing students to piece it together alone.

🗺️

No Actionable Roadmap

Existing tools suggest career domains but fail to provide the step-by-step plan: which exams to take, when to prepare, and which colleges to target.

Problem Statement
"Choosing an appropriate career path is a complex and critical decision for students, often influenced by limited information, societal pressure, and lack of personalized guidance. Although several career recommendation systems exist, they primarily offer career suggestions without actionable guidance on how to achieve them."

This project bridges the gap by delivering personalized recommendations plus a detailed roadmap of competitive exams and comparative college analysis.

Research Gap

What Existing Systems
Get Wrong

Our literature survey identified three critical gaps that this project directly addresses.

01

No Actionable Roadmaps

Most models stop at suggesting a career domain and do not provide structured roadmaps with relevant competitive exams, exam timelines, or preparation stages.

02

No College Integration

Current solutions rarely integrate college-level comparisons based on cut-offs, rankings, fees, or placement statistics — forcing users to rely on multiple external platforms.

03

Static & Non-Adaptive

Many systems do not adapt to changing exam patterns, eligibility criteria, or evolving job market trends — leading to outdated and inaccurate recommendations.

04

Generic Suggestions

Existing CF, hybrid, and content-based models often produce generalised suggestions for ambiguous user profiles, lacking the depth needed for truly personalised guidance.
